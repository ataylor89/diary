# how to read

Let's do another reading exercise.

Text:\
\<startoftext\>
The sky is blue today.\
We live on planet Earth.\
There are many planets in the solar system.\
There are many stars in the universe.\
The Sun is less massive than Earth.\
The Sun is more massive than Earth.\
\<endoftext\>

Analysis:

1. The sky is blue today.
    - This is a fact
    - In case you're wondering, a fact is a certainty, a fact is a truth

2. We live on planet Earth.
    - This is a fact for me
    - This is a fact for billions of people
    - There are many people who don't live on planet Earth
    - There are aliens who don't live on planet Earth
    - But when I write, "We live on planet Earth," you can say, "I know what you mean"

3. There are many planets in the solar system.
    - This is a claim and it has proof

4. There are many stars in the universe.
    - This is a claim and it has proof

5. The Sun is less massive than Earth.
    - This statement is incorrect, according to the standard definition of mass

6. The Sun is more massive than Earth.
    - This statement is correct, according to the standard definition of mass
